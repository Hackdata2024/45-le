During the pendency of a suit by a person claiming to be the heir of the Bettiah estate which was in the possession of the Manager, Court of Wards, the Income Tax Officer made an assessment on the Manager.
In those proceedings the State of Bihar and the Manager claimed that the estate had vested in the State by escheat and therefore the income was not liable to tax.
The Income tax Officer, the Appellate Assistant Commissioner and the Tribunal held that as the litigation was pending it could not be said of the estate that the same had vested in the State by escheat.
The High Court on reference held that the income tax authorities could not impose the tax.
Setting aside the judgment of the High Court this Court in appeal.
HELD : The proceedings should be made final after the disposal of the litigation and the High Court could call for a supplementary statement of the case if necessary [750 D]
